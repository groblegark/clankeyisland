# Punch-Up Sweep — 2026-06-12 (Comedy Chair)

Showrunner-requested sweep. Trigger: the invoice line in docks.scc ("how is the
rattling bolt like an invoice? i don't get it"). Exhibit A leads; the rest of the
sweep hunts its failure class (missing-step jokes) plus flats, weak punch words,
explained jokes, and same-shape fatigue (charter budget: <=2 uses per construction).

Ground rules observed: every proposal is a 1:1 string replacement (no say lines
added, removed, reordered, or split — dub pairing is source-order-dependent),
ASCII only, <130 chars, narrator stays deadpan and never performs. Settled
dispositions from NOTES-2026-06-12.md and -pass2.md are not re-litigated; two
notes below ENFORCE a settled disposition rather than reopen it. The showrunner
disposes — nothing here is applied.

## EXHIBIT A

### P-D1. The invoice line compresses out its middle step
- **File:line:** `game/docks.scc:163` (neonSign:LookAt, pre-whack branch, third line)
- **Current line (verbatim):**
  `"Something inside it rattles on every flicker. Loose hardware. Practically an invoice."`
- **Failure class:** MISSING-STEP
- **Why it fails:** The intended chain is rattle -> loose hardware -> *a repair
  job somebody could do* -> *which gets billed* -> invoice. The line jumps from
  step 2 to step 4. "Invoice" only parses if the player supplies "repair work is
  billable" themselves, and nothing in the line points at work — an invoice is
  what arrives AFTER a job, so the punchline names the paperwork of an event the
  line never establishes. The showrunner couldn't reconstruct it; neither will a
  player. Note the constraint on any fix: this line is also the B1 breadcrumb
  (added per pass-1 NIT) that points the player at whacking the sign — "loose
  thing inside the sign, shakeable" must stay legible. All three alternatives
  preserve it.
- **Proposed rewrites (alternatives, pick one):**
  1. `"Something inside it rattles on every flicker. Loose hardware. That's not a noise, that's a work order."`
     — restores the missing step by making the step itself the punchline: a
     rattle directly generates a work order, no billing inference required.
     Ends on the funny words. Maintenance-bot register exactly. **(high)**
  2. `"Something inside it rattles on every flicker. Loose hardware. Where I'm from, that sound is billable."`
     — keeps the original money angle but routes it through Sprocket's trade
     reflex ("where I'm from" = the profession supplies the missing step).
     Ends on "billable". **(high)**
  3. `"Something inside it rattles on every flicker. Loose hardware. A job, waiting. The city just doesn't know it's hiring."`
     — full rebuild: spells the step ("a job, waiting") then turns municipal.
     Slightly longer breath; strongest hint value for B1, softest joke. **(med)**
- **Confidence:** high (that it fails); rewrite preference order as numbered.

## docks.scc

### P-D2. "Legal tender" is told twice within seconds of itself
- **File:line:** `game/docks.scc:214` (dockBolt:LookAt)
- **Current line (verbatim):** `"A bolt! Genuine legal tender of Clanker City."`
- **Failure class:** SAME-SHAPE (verbatim-kin duplicate) + register (exclamation comedy)
- **Why it fails:** The golden path is look at floor bolt -> pick up -> examine in
  inventory, and the inventory LookAt (`inventoryitems.scc:27`) is "One genuine
  Clanker City bolt. Legal tender, light snack, structural member." Same joke,
  same "genuine ... legal tender" wording, minutes — often seconds — apart. This
  is the exact N-I2 disease (two drafts of one joke both shipped), and the
  inventory version is strictly better (escalating list). The floor version also
  carries the act's only narrator exclamation point left standing. The
  currency-teaching function must survive the rewrite.
- **Proposed rewrite:** `"A bolt. On the ground it's litter. In my pocket it's capital."`
- **Confidence:** high

### P-D3. The ferry's punch word fires first, then the line trails off
- **File:line:** `game/docks.scc:237` (ferry:LookAt)
- **Current line (verbatim):** `"The S.S. Eventually. My ride here, and apparently nothing's ride anywhere ever again."`
- **Failure class:** WEAK PUNCH WORDS
- **Why it fails:** "The S.S. Eventually" is the joke, and it's the first three
  words; everything after is a wobbly clause ("apparently nothing's ride anywhere
  ever again") that ends on "again" — connective mush after the laugh. The line
  should let the name land, then add a second, smaller turn that ends funny.
- **Proposed rewrite:** `"The S.S. Eventually. The name was a schedule. Now it's a diagnosis."`
- **Confidence:** med

## midtown.scc

### P-M1. The aide gag lands, then Sprocket grades the door
- **File:line:** `game/midtown.scc:314` (cityHall:UsedWith poster, third line)
- **Current line (verbatim):** `"Somebody in there is afraid of this piece of paper. Good. So am I, a little."`
- **Failure class:** EXPLAINED
- **Why it fails:** The beat's joke is line two: "Then he closes the door eight

  riddles aren't funny." and "Tough room." cut in prior passes. The story plant
  (the notice is a weapon; GDD: "the official notice frightens his aides") must
  survive, so the replacement keeps the weapon framing without restating the
  inference the door just performed.
- **Proposed rewrite:** `"A whole government, one degree of open. The paper goes back in my pocket, loaded."`
- **Confidence:** med

- **Current line (verbatim):** `"I can't do that!"`
- **Failure class:** FLAT (+ register: narrator exclamation)
