# Scene 02 — The Scrap & Barrel Tavern

**Act:** 1 · **Background:** `assets/backgrounds/scrap-and-barrel.png` (320x200)

Dockside tavern where robots come to knock back a pint of 10W-40. Dim, warm,
full of cigarette-lighter-flame mood lighting. A player piano plays itself —
badly, because it's missing three keys (foreshadowing: *keys*).

## Layout

1. **Bar** — Bartender **Gusket** polishes the same glass forever (his arm
   servo is stuck in a loop).
2. **The Rustlers' corner table** — three corroded pirate-bots playing cards
   with washers. Eavesdropping here reveals the hideout's knock-code.
3. **Player piano** — interactive; the missing-keys gag.
4. **Dartboard** — minigame; win an **oil can** off a dockworker bot.
5. **Back door** — locked; leads to the Rustlers' alley (Scene 03) once the
   knock-code is learned.

## Key interactions

| Hotspot | Notes |
|---|---|
| Gusket | Dialog hub. Quest: fix his stuck servo (use **bolt** as a shim) → free drink token → trade token to dockworker for a dart game |
| Dartboard | Simple 3-round minigame. Player darts are magnetic; the secret is to *lose* badly enough that the dockworker shows off and overheats → he forfeits the **oil can** |
| Rustlers' table | Stand close 10s → overhear knock-code (random 4-beat pattern per save). Get caught twice → comic bounce-out, re-enter freely |
| Player piano | Examine: "It's missing three keys." Sprocket: "...That feels thematically significant." |
| Back door | Use knock-code → Scene 03 (Rustlers' alley) |

## Writing notes

- Gusket speaks only in bartender clichés with mechanical substitutions
  ("You look like you've had a hard drive, pal").
- The Rustlers argue about whether washers count as money. One insists
  they're "post-bolt currency."
